## Post 1
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_250-sales-teams-10000-reps-heres-the-share-7470853432799698946-_2rj

250 sales teams. 10,000 reps. Here's the #1 thing killing cold outreach results:

Starting emails with "I" or "We."

"I wanted to reach out..."
"We help companies like yours..."
"I came across your profile..."

The prospect doesn't care about you yet. They care about themselves.

Flip it. Start with them:
- A problem they're likely facing
- A result someone like them got
- A trigger event specific to their company

The first line is the only line that matters until they decide to keep reading. Make it about them.

## Post 2
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_i-disagree-that-the-first-line-of-a-cold-activity-7468678070321770497-EtBb

I disagree that the first line of a cold email needs to be a compliment or "personalized opener."

That advice is outdated.

Prospects have seen "I loved your recent post about X" a thousand times. It signals a template, not genuine interest.

What actually works as an opener in 2026:
- Lead with a specific outcome you drove for someone similar
- Reference a real trigger (funding, hiring, product launch)
- Ask a question they actually want to answer

Personalization isn't about flattery. It's about relevance.

## Post 3
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_are-we-really-still-starting-emails-with-activity-7465086532509405184-yqhN

Are we really still starting emails with "Hope this finds you well"?

Every generic opener is a signal to the prospect: this email wasn't written for me.

The REPLY Method for cold email openers:
- **R**elevance — why them, why now
- **E**motion — connect to a real pain
- **P**ersonalization — something specific to their world
- **L**evity — keep it human, not corporate
- **Y**ou — make it about them, not you

One good opener does more work than five follow-up emails.

## Post 4 — The Outbound Equation
Most reps focus 90% of their energy on copy and messaging.

But messaging is only one variable.

The Outbound Equation:
- Right person (ICP fit)
- Right time (buying trigger)
- Right message (relevant, specific)
- Right channel (where they actually are)
- Right frequency (enough touches, not spam)

You can have the best email in the world and still get no replies if you're sending it to the wrong person at the wrong time.

Fix targeting first. Then messaging. In that order.
